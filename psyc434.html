Questions for Article Critique

Section 1 - Introduction

  • Is the terminology (language) consistent with the claim? (Association vs Direct Link)
    • Have they confused correlation with causation?
  • What is the foundation of the research? Is the relationship between the following foundational elements consistent, clear and precise?
    • theoretical foundation/sources
    • goals/purpose
    • research question
    • selection & measurement of variables
      • Why did they choose to test these specific variables?
      • Is the causal logic consistent?
  • Is the use of citations linked well to what is being addressed? (Self-citations, relevance, how recent, causal logic)

Section 2 - Overview of Study / Methods

  • What can the methods and measurements used tell us about the assumptions of the study and its researchers?
  • Are there any assumptions or conclusions made within the study that go unsupported by data or other studies?
  • Are there any unwarranted extrapolations in the study?
    • (For example, have the authors made any unjustified links between concepts or between evidence and claims?)​

Section 4 - Discussion

  • What kind of relationship did they find, if any? How do you know?
  • How can these findings be generalized to other studies?
  • Does it have internal or external validity?
    • How could they have improved internal and/or external validity?
  • What conclusions were they able to arrive to?
  • Were all of the results addressed in the discussion? Why?
    • If data was left out of the discussion, how does that affect your view of the results?
  • How might you have done things differently?
  • How might the study have been approached and executed from and alternative methodological standpoint?
  • Focus - Have the authors adequately addressed data which may contradict their hypothesis?
    • EX. Did the author discuss findings which may cause issue with their hypothesis or did they neglect to mention or explore certain aspects of their results?
  • Perspective - What perspective is the author taking and how might that affect the author’s interpretation of the data. Have other disciplinary perspectives been considered?
    • EX. The author is using a particular perspective to interpret the results and possibly ignoring other perspectives/implications for the results.
  • Limitations - In the limitations section, has the author clearly explored the limitations of the study and do these limitations contradict the study’s findings.
    • EX. The emphasis on causality throughout the article and then mentioning that the data cannot necessarily prove causality.
  • Contribution - Has the article contributed to the world of science or to real-world application?
    • EX. Where the results useful/helpful in the progression of science or application in the world?
